# Chapter 01 — Information Security Fundamentals

## Overview

Introduces the foundational principles of Information Security and their practical application in an organizational context.

The concepts presented in this chapter are applied through a structured deliverable developed for **Mandavi FinTech Pvt. Ltd.**, a **fictional fintech company created solely for educational and portfolio purposes**. The organization and its assets are hypothetical and are used to simulate real-world GRC practices.

---

## Topics Covered

* CIA Triad — Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability
* Information Assets and Asset Classification
* Asset Owners and Asset Lifecycle
* Asset Inventory Concepts
* Security Objectives and Principles
* Types of Information — Structured vs Unstructured

---

## Practical Exercise

Developed an **Information Asset Inventory** for the fictional organization **Mandavi FinTech Pvt. Ltd.**, identifying and classifying organizational assets across data, hardware, software, network, cloud infrastructure, and business process categories.

Each asset is mapped against its owner, classification level, business value, CIA priority, and location to demonstrate fundamental asset management practices used in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC).

---

## Deliverable

**Information Asset Inventory** — An Excel workbook containing **17 hypothetical organizational assets** classified by asset type, owner, classification, business value, CIA priority, and location.
